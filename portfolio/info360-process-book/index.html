Driver Process Book

INFO 360 C Winter 2015

By Alex Bieg

Abstract

This process book takes you through my expericence making a carpooling application called Driver. It will take you through each step of the process and explain our reasoning behind it. If you would like to skip around, the navigation bar at the top will take you to the noted section. This was done for INFO 360.

Issue

Our first challenge was to come up with a problem to solve. It had to relate to surviving college, but nothing else was really specified. We started thinking about things currently in our lives that we did not like. I mentioned I could never decide where to get food. We talked about that for awhile, but ultimately decided there were already many applications that solved that issue. As the class was nearly over Huy mentioned how he was going to have to ride the bus home today because his carpool buddy was not able to drive that day. I think this struck a chord with Saki and me. All college students have been trying to get somewhere, but have to take the bus or walk because they do not have a car.

From here we started to define our issue. UW students have 4 major potential ways of getting to campus. First is the bus. UW students have the Upass so there is not a monetary barrier, but if you live in Bellevue for instance, the bus to get here only runs every 30 minutes. Second is walking. This works perfectly fine if you live close to campust, but our research shows that more than 11 thousand students live too far to walk. Third is just owning your own car. This is probably the best solution in terms of features, but owning a car is expensive and finding a place to park can be challenging. Last is to join a carpool. This is often a great comprimise between walking and owning a car, but people are often unreliable and you do not know who you will be riding with. So we decided to improve on carpools.

Idea

So from that issue we create the basic idea. We wanted to create a carpool app. We were not sure what our scope should be. At first we wanted to make an app that allowed students to go anywhere, not just campus, but as we talked to our professor and TA it was clear that we should narrow our scope. So filtered it down to just students going to and from campus. Specifically we wanted to serve the seattle campus. This meant that any student who attends a different campus such as Tacoma would be out of scope for our idea. From here we went into our ideation phase to determine what features we wanted our application to have.

To confirm that this idea would resonate with our userbase we decided to conduct a few interviews. Each of us chose one person to interview. We prepared a loose set of questions and tried to let the person speak honestly about our proposed idea. In the end we came back together and synthisized our responses. On the whole people were positve about the idea we had. In light of this we moved forward with our idea.

Ideation

At this point in the process we all went off individually and came up with 40 ways we could create this idea. This is where Bryce joined us. It was significantly harder than I expected to come up with that many ideas, but in the end I made it. Below is are the ideas I came up with. Please excuse the bad drawings. We were allowed to come up with crazy idea just to get our brains flowing with the creative process. So some of these ideas are unrealistic. Two of my ideas really stuck out to me. The schedule matcher which automatcally matched up people who had similar schedules and the same class study session which allowed people in the same class to register carpools so that they could study for that class on the way to school.

Choosing

This is where the team as a whole met back up to choose our favorite ideas out of the ones we created. This was somewhat of a tall order because collectively we had created 160 ideas. To save all of us time and a headache we had each person choose their favorite ideas out of the ones they had create to present to the group. I chose the two I mentioned above. Huy wanted to have parking spots designated for carpools, because parking in the University District can be very difficult or expensive. We all thought this was a great idea and decided to add it to our plan. I presented my two ideas. The group really liked the schedule matcher, so we added it. They were less than enthused about the study idea. I tried to argue for it, but in the end I had to let it go. Bryce came up with the idea to make everyone who logged in prove they were a UW student by using UW NetId. We all thought that was a great idea. Lastly Saki wanted to create a service that catered to professors as well, but we wanted to keep our scope small for this project. So we did not move forward with her idea.

Rethinking

At this point Bryce asked us how happy we were with our idea. Since he came in late he did not really have any input into what our issue was. He was not really excited by making a carpooling app and wanted to switch to something else. In particular he wanted to switch to an app to encourage students to vote more. He said this fit with our current goals because students voting more would mean that public transportation would fit our needs better.

Saki was opposed to this idea because she is not able to vote in the US. This type of app would not serve someone like her in any way. Huy was very much in favor of the idea. I said I did not want to switch focuses now, because we had already done so much work on our current topic. At this everyone agreed so we stayed with our carpool idea.

Reflection

In the end I am very happy with our product. I think we created something could actually be helpful to many people. Really in creating something that is the goal. Make something that will be used by people. In our case I think, given enough time to implement it, our app would be used. We had solid evidence that there was a need for it. We ideated many ideas. We went through multiple interations of screens. We user tested many times. We worked really hard on this project. I think we made something awesome. I hope you do too.
