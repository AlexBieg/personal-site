The entire song was composed by algorithms. This may not be too hard to believe considering how inhuman it sounded, but I think that is insanely cool. The whole process works in series. There are 12 speakers that are hooked up in a line. Each one has it’s own specific algorithm. The first one sends its data to the second, the second to the third and so on. They all have ways they are programmed to interact with the data.

Some might drop the note by an octave. Another might add beats in between the current beats. By the end of the cycle each device will be creating it’s own music in concert (pun intended) with the others.

The article mentions that the music will drastically change depending on who wrote the algorithm, so it is worth noting that human interaction is still needed for different sounds. I think this brings up an interesting point. The creators called this an automatic orchestra, but it still needs developers to change its sound. So how automatic is it really. I suppose that is a matter of opinion.

The entire idea of music created by non-sentient objects is very strange. Most if not all music is created to elicit emotions. How can something without emotions try to create them in others? Well as some of you know music is much more mathematical than you might imagine. Each octave has properties the same as the next. Notes work in chords because of wavelengths synching up. So it is not totally crazy that a device whose entire job is to calculate things might be able to make music.

I will be honest I am not a huge fan of most of the music it created. This doesn’t mean I don’t think it is impressive and very exciting. I am just too picky. I hope this trend continues and more and more music is created this way. I would love to see how the musical environment changes as the beings who make it change as well.
